In 2000, Netflix offered to sell itself to Blockbuster for fifty million dollars. Blockbuster's CEO literally laughed them out of the room.

At its peak, Blockbuster had nine thousand stores and millions of customers. But their business model relied on a dark secret: late fees. When Netflix launched with no late fees and unlimited rentals, Blockbuster ignored them. By the time they realized their mistake and launched an online service, Netflix was already transitioning to streaming. Blockbuster filed for bankruptcy in 2010.
